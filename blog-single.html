Why force yourself to work full-time until your 60s if you can afford not to?

  • Sergio Torres
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

Let's introduce the FIRE financial movement (Financial Independence Retire Early). The common goal of FIRE is to have a liquid net worth that’s 25 times higher than your annual living expenses.

Most people who achieve FIRE don’t retire full-time. Most find a vocation that’s more fulfilling and less time-intensive.

Stock market investing and owning real estate play a pivotal role for most FIRE-seekers.

The 3 essential habits for anyone to reach FIRE are:

1. Extreme savings,

2. A lot of hard work

3. Aggressive investing.

How you earn your income, where you invest your cash, and how much you spend each month is up to you.

Also, you don’t need to be in your 20s or 30s to start pursuing FIRE. People in their 40s and 50s are joining the FIRE movement too.

“Rule of 25.” Simply put, your liquid assets are 25 times higher than your annual expenses. If your annual expenses are $50,000, you need $1.25 million in liquid investments.

the other rule assumes your investment portfolio earns a 7% annual return and the inflation rate is 3%. Portfolio growth minus inflation leaves you with 4% of the earnings to spend each year.

Three Most Common Types of FIRE:

1. FatFIRE: At least 50 times assets to annual expenses

2. Regular FIRE: At least 25 times assets to annual expenses

3. LeanFIRE: Your liquid assets don’t exceed $1 million and your annual living expenses are less than $40,000

How You Can Start Saving for FIRE

All of us are living a different journey and have different goals. If you want to challenge the retirement status quo, here are few stepping stones to start chasing FIRE.

Increase your earned income. Look for ways to increase your annual income at work. A second idea is launching a side hustle with the potential to scale into a business.

Reduce your expenses. You don’t need to live like a college student but try to reduce unnecessary spending and delay large purchases when possible.

Invest your extra income. Invest your newly found extra income. You can earn more passive income. Index funds are a low-cost staple in most FIRE portfolios but invest in what interests you most.

Celebrate small victories. To avoid burnout and frustration, celebrate minor successes. Maybe you take a mini-vacation each time your net worth increases by 1%.

Review your FIRE goals periodically. Take time to remember the reason why you’re pursuing a certain FIRE level. Don’t be afraid to slow down if the short-term sacrifice is too much.

Pursuing FIRE is a worthy goal to maximize your long-term goals. Most people no longer have the option to “retire early” at 55 and collect a pension. As a result, being smart with your money gives you career and lifestyle flexibility.
